Welcome to Huntington Beach

Huntington Beach is a popular California City southeast of Los Angeles. It's known for surf beaches, such as Huntington City Beach, home of the US Open. The International Surfing Museum displays longboards and other memorabilia. Sprawling Huntington Central Park features fields, gardens and lakes, plus an equestrian center. Bolsa Chica Ecological Reserve's wetlands and dunes shelter h ndreds of bird species.

Located in Southern California between Los Angeles and San Diego, with three airports near Huntington Beach. Surf City USA 10 miles/16 kilometers wide open sandy beaches make it a paradise for families, couples, dogs, and surfers. With Cali's famous endless summer weather offers relaxation, things to do, and oceanfront dining year-round. Some visitors' favorites include biking, beach bonfire pits, kayaking, and of course, the iconic Huntington Beach Pier spanning 1,850 feet / 0.56 kilometers in length. Huntington Beaches pier sits in the heart of Huntington Beach and allows visitors to experience breathtaking views amidst the sounds of crashing waves and the ocean breeze.

Made up of 10 miles of uninterrupted beaches, Huntington Beach offers luxurious oceanfront resorts along Pacific Coast Highway with relaxation amenities, outdoor activities, culinary experiences, and almost everything in between.

It's stretch of sand sits along the Pacific Coast Highway (PCH) between Goldenwest St. and Beach Blvd, and it’s a great chance to see professional surfers surf while you catch some sun. Huntington City Beach offers various amenities such as volleyball courts, bonfire pits, and of course the iconic Huntington Beach Pier spanning 1,850 feet in length. The pier sits in the heart of Huntington Beach and allows visitors and locals alike to experience the breathtaking views amidst the sounds of crashing waves and the ocean breeze. Yu can even enjoy an oceanfront view of Huntington City Beach at one of Huntington Beach's oceanfront hotels.

Surf Culture

Surfing, like so many outdoor pursuits, has surged during the pandemic, as more people have picked up longboards and signed up for lessons. Many of these new surfers are kids or teenagers, such as Caitlin Simmers, 16, an Oceanside, California, local who has been quickly moving up the competitive ranks of the sport. “During the pandemic, [surfing] was one of the only things you could do, so now there’s more people in the water,” says Caitlin’s mom, Ali Simmers. With sandy breaks, beachside parks, and tide pools for smaller children to explore—not to mention fewer crowds and consistent swells in the winter—Southern California is an ideal place for families to learn to surf year-round. And the presence there of a growing number of young surfers mastering the waves serves as inspiration.
